## 0. The Intervention Record

The literature on intellectual humility, epistemic justice, and organizational bias is not a literature without interventions. It has produced IH-elevation protocols, bias-awareness trainings, structural reforms to credentialing systems, relational reconfiguration programs, and procedural redesigns aimed at reducing the arrogance attribution's toll on peripheral contributors. Some of these interventions show measurable effects in controlled conditions. The mechanism they target returns.

This is not an accident of implementation quality. It is not a failure of scale or duration or institutional commitment. It is the predictable output of a structural property that the intervention literature has not yet formally named.

The arrogance attribution mechanism — the Charged Vacancy, generated by the Instrument Switch's concurrent approach and avoidance axes, maintained by the Sincerity Paradox's phenomenological concealment, the Intensity Paradox's apparent authority, and the Dissolution Paradox's suppression of the corrective faculty — is not a mechanism with independent vulnerabilities. It is a mechanism whose apparent vulnerabilities are integrated defenses. The three paradoxes at its core are not co-present properties that happen to coexist. They protect each other. When one is disrupted, the remaining two reconstitute the mechanism's immunity using the disruption itself as a new input.

The Triad Coherence Criterion (TCC) is the formal statement of this property: single-paradox interventions cannot produce durable dissolution of the arrogance attribution mechanism because the paradoxes are integrated. Disrupting one while leaving the others intact allows the remaining architecture to rebuild immunity — often more quickly and more thoroughly than the mechanism's original production, because the intervention has activated the social and identity-protective processes that the remaining paradoxes feed on.

The TCC is falsifiable. If interventions targeting single paradoxes show equivalent durable efficacy to integrated interventions, the unity claim is disconfirmed. This document makes the case for the unity claim, specifies the reconstitution mechanism for each single-paradox disruption attempt, identifies the minimum intervention set required to cross the TCC's threshold, and generates the empirically testable predictions that would distinguish the TCC's account from alternative accounts in which the paradoxes are independent.

## I. The Integration Claim: Why the Paradoxes Are Not Independent

The prior frameworks established what each paradox is. THE-INVERTED-SIGNAL established that the three paradoxes constitute an architecture in which each reinforces the structural function of the other two. The integration claim requires a more precise account of the mechanism of integration — not merely that the paradoxes co-exist, but why disrupting one paradox allows the others to reconstitute the mechanism around the disruption.

### I.1 The Shared Generative Substrate

The three paradoxes are not independent generators. They arise from a shared mechanism: the Double Response Signature's concurrent approach and avoidance axes, activated by status threat in the CPT zone.

The approach axis produces the IP's charge: status defense energy transferred to the attribution via the Authority Transfer, presenting as apparent evaluative intensity.

The avoidance axis produces the SP's concealment substrate: the IS's complete substitution of social evaluation for domain evaluation, generating the Vacant Core that runs below the threshold of introspective access.

The RIHG — the systematic suppression of IH in hierarchical CPT-zone configurations — produces the DP's suppression: the corrective faculty most capable of interrupting both approach and avoidance axes is unavailable exactly where the DRS fires hardest.

All three paradoxes are outputs of the same mechanism: the DRS/IS operating under status threat in the CPT zone. This shared substrate is why they are integrated rather than independent. They are not three separate problems that happen to co-occur. They are three simultaneous expressions of one problem.

### I.2 The Protection Architecture

The integration is not only generative — it is protective. Each paradox actively maintains the conditions under which the other two operate.

**SP maintains IP's social function:** The IP's apparent authority depends on the attribution being delivered with genuine phenomenological certainty. If the accuser were visibly uncertain — if the SP were absent — bystanders would discount the intensity as performance. The attribution would carry the social weight of posturing, not assessment. The SP's concealment ensures the accuser's sincerity is authentic, which is what makes the IP's charge land as authority rather than bluster.

**IP maintains SP's concealment:** A brief, low-intensity attribution invites revisitation — by the accuser, by bystanders, by the epistemic community. The encounter remains open. The SP's concealment has an easier job in an open encounter: the accuser hasn't committed enough energy to feel the cost of revising. A high-intensity attribution closes the encounter. The commitment of the approach axis forecloses the reflective access the SP relies on being unavailable. The IP's charge is the closure mechanism that protects the SP's self-concealment from outside disruption.

**DP protects both:** The IH that would produce meta-awareness of the IS (dissolving SP from inside) and reduce approach-axis activation (dissolving IP from its source) is suppressed by the RIHG in exactly the hierarchical CPT-zone configuration where SP and IP are most active. The DP does not merely withhold the corrective. It witholds the corrective at the moment of maximum need, where the SP's concealment and the IP's charge are operating at maximum intensity.

This protection architecture is what makes the integration claim more than a claim about co-occurrence. The paradoxes are mutually sustaining. Each is a condition for the other two's continued operation. This is what the TCC formalizes.

## II. The Reconstitution Mechanism

When a single-paradox intervention disrupts one element of the triad, the remaining two paradoxes do not remain static. They respond to the disruption. In most cases, they respond by intensifying — because the disruption itself activates the same DRS processes that generated the mechanism originally.

The Reconstitution Mechanism (RM) names this dynamic: the process by which the remaining paradoxes rebuild the mechanism's immunity after single-paradox disruption, using the disruption as an input that strengthens the surviving elements. The RM has a specific structure for each disruption type.

### II.1 When SP Is Disrupted

**The disruption:** The accuser gains some introspective access to the IS. Meta-cognitive training, reflective practice, or a confrontation with the substitution mechanism has partially opened the SP's concealment. The accuser recognizes, in the aftermath of the attribution, that they may have been issuing social evaluation rather than domain evaluation.

**IP's reconstitution pathway:** The attribution has already been delivered. Its intensity has already charged the bystander community's perception. Kelley's covariation model shows the consequence: the consensus dimension has been populated — multiple observers received the attribution with apparent authority, many said nothing (implicit endorsement), some affirmed it explicitly. This consensus is not undone by the accuser's subsequent reflective recognition. The social verdict exists independently of the accuser's subsequent epistemic state. The IP's product — the bystander consensus that the attribution represents careful evaluation — persists even after the SP that authenticated it has been partially disrupted.

**DP's reconstitution pathway:** The SP's partial disruption does not alter the relational architecture that produces the RIHG. The hierarchical configuration remains. The status threat dynamic remains. Future CPT-zone encounters in the same relational structure will reactivate the full DRS, and the RIHG will suppress IH before the accuser can deploy the meta-awareness their prior SP disruption provided. The reflective capacity gained in the aftermath of one encounter is available in contexts of low threat and high IH. It is not available in the high-threat, low-IH configuration of the next CPT-zone encounter, which is when it would be needed.

**Result:** The SP disruption produces genuine reflective capacity in the interval between encounters — in conditions of low threat and high IH availability. The IP's product (bystander consensus) persists from the prior encounter. The DP's RIHG reconstitutes the full SP in the next encounter. The mechanism regenerates.

### II.2 When IP Is Disrupted

**The disruption:** Bystanders have been trained to discount attribution intensity as an authority signal. Audience education, epistemic norms reform, or institutional culture change has reduced the IP's social authority function. The attribution is still delivered with the same intensity, but bystanders no longer convert intensity into endorsement.

**SP's reconstitution pathway:** Nothing in the IP disruption affects the phenomenological register. The accuser continues to experience the attribution as accurate perception. The IS continues to run below deliberate awareness. The SP's concealment is fully intact. Without the social endorsement that IP produced, the accuser receives no bystander confirmation — but the SP generates internal confirmation: the accuser's subjective certainty. This certainty does not require bystander endorsement to sustain itself. The SP is sufficient to maintain the accuser's commitment to the attribution in the absence of external confirmation.

**DP's reconstitution pathway:** The IP disruption has removed one social reward for the attribution (bystander authority) but has not altered the status threat that drives the DRS. In some cases, the removal of bystander endorsement increases the accuser's social frustration — the categorical pair enforcement mechanism has been partially disabled, which may itself function as a status threat input (the established node's social enforcement tools are not working). This secondary status threat activates the DRS again. The DP's RIHG continues to suppress IH in the hierarchical configuration, ensuring the corrective faculty remains unavailable.

**IP's reconstitution pathway:** The audience training that disrupted IP's authority function operates at the bystander perception level. It does not alter the approach axis's output — the attribution continues to arrive with the energy of status defense. In new social contexts (new bystander communities, new institutional environments), the IP training has not traveled. The attribution mechanism has not changed. When the accuser encounters a new social environment, IP reconstitutes immediately, because the approach-axis charge persists and new audiences have not received the disrupting training.

**Result:** Within trained communities, the IP disruption reduces the mechanism's social amplification but does not dissolve its psychological operation. The SP maintains the accuser's internal certainty. The DP maintains IH suppression. When the social context changes — new bystanders, new institutions — IP reconstitutes completely. The mechanism persists in its generator; only its specific social expression has been modified.

### II.3 When DP Is Disrupted

**The disruption:** The relational architecture has been restructured to reduce the RIHG. Hierarchy flattening, peer-status reframing, or structural IH induction has made IH more accessible to the established node in CPT-zone encounters. The DP's suppression mechanism has been partially disabled.

**SP's reconstitution pathway:** Higher IH availability means the established node is, in principle, more open to revising beliefs and recognizing limitations. But IH is a general disposition toward epistemic openness. The SP's concealment operates at the specific level of the IS's output — the mechanism that produces the attribution runs below deliberate awareness in a way that high IH is not automatically positioned to intercept. A perceiver with elevated IH in general can still experience the IS's output as accurate perception, because the IS's concealment does not operate at the level of belief revision (where IH would help) but at the level of the perception-generation process (where IH, without specific meta-awareness of the IS, does not automatically apply). The SP reconstitutes by remaining in the perceptual register where IH's general openness cannot reach it without specific IS-directed training.

**IP's reconstitution pathway:** The DP disruption restores IH access but does not reduce the approach axis's charge. The status threat continues to activate approach-axis activity. The Authority Transfer continues to charge the attribution with defense energy. Without a concurrent IP disruption, bystanders continue to receive the IP's apparent-authority signal and continue to convert intensity into endorsement. The IP reconstitutes because its generative mechanism — the approach axis — has not been altered by the DP disruption.

**DP's partial reconstitution pathway:** The RIHG suppression is relational. Structural changes that reduce it in controlled conditions (experimental meetings, specifically redesigned encounter formats) may not generalize to uncontrolled encounters in the same hierarchical environment. The hierarchical configuration is the primary driver of RIHG. If the structure of the institution remains hierarchical, the RIHG reconstitutes as the controlled condition becomes the ambient condition.

**Result:** DP disruption is the most proximate single-paradox disruption — it restores the corrective faculty. But it is insufficient alone because SP prevents the restored faculty from recognizing what it needs to correct, and IP continues to charge the attribution with apparent authority that bystanders endorse. The mechanism's concealment and social amplification both persist. DP disruption alone produces a perceiver who is more epistemically open in general but continues to experience the specific IS substitution as accurate perception.

## III. The Paradox Dominance Ordering

The three single-paradox disruptions are not equivalent in proximity to the dissolution threshold. The RM analysis reveals a dominance ordering: DP disruption is most proximate to durable dissolution, IP disruption is least proximate, and SP disruption occupies the intermediate position.

### III.1 Why DP Dominance Is Not Sufficient

DP disruption is most proximate because it restores the corrective faculty — the specific faculty (IH) that, deployed fully, would interrupt both remaining paradoxes. High IH deployed toward the functional outgroup member in the CPT zone would reduce approach-axis activation (reducing IP's charge source) and produce meta-awareness of the IS (dissolving SP from inside). The DP's disruption targets the mechanism at the level of its antidote rather than at the level of its outputs.

But the DP disruption alone crosses neither of the two remaining paradoxes' thresholds. SP's concealment operates in the perceptual register; restored IH is positioned in the belief-revision register. A perceiver with elevated IH can recognize their general epistemic limits while experiencing the specific IS substitution as accurate perception. IP's approach-axis charge is unaffected by DP disruption; the attribution continues to arrive with defense energy that bystanders receive as evaluative authority.

**The DP dominance paradox:** Restoring the corrective faculty does not automatically deploy it. The faculty can be restored without becoming operative in the specific register where the mechanism runs.

### III.2 Why IP Disruption Is Least Proximate

IP disruption targets the mechanism's social amplification — the bystander perception process that converts intensity into endorsement. It is least proximate because the mechanism's generative core (the DRS/IS) is entirely unaffected. The attribution mechanism continues to operate at full capacity; only one downstream effect has been modified. The mechanism continues to generate confident attributions. It continues to conceive of them as accurate perception (SP intact). The IH that would correct it remains suppressed (DP intact). The only thing that changes is the social amplification of the output — which is itself the mechanism's most distal and most context-dependent property.

**The IP disruption asymmetry:** IP disruption produces the most visible short-term change (reduced community endorsement) while leaving the generative mechanism most intact, producing the fastest and most complete reconstitution when social context changes.

## IV. The Minimum Intervention Set

The TCC's positive claim: durable dissolution requires addressing at least two paradoxes simultaneously, or addressing the DRS that generates all three. The negative claim (that single-paradox interventions fail) has been established. The positive claim requires specifying which two-paradox combinations are viable and which is most tractable.

### IV.1 The Three Two-Paradox Combinations

**SP + DP:**
SP disruption provides meta-awareness of the IS. DP disruption restores IH access. Together: the perceiver has IH available (DP) and has specific IS-awareness to direct it toward (SP). The combination dissolves the mechanism's concealment from both inside and from the corrective faculty's direction. Without IP intact to charge the social amplification, the attribution loses its bystander authority function — not because bystanders have been trained (IP disruption) but because the accuser, now possessing IS-awareness and IH access, does not issue the attribution with the approach-axis commitment that IP requires as its input.

SP + DP is likely the most tractable combination because disrupting SP (via IS-targeted metacognitive training) and disrupting DP (via relational restructuring) address the mechanism at the level of the accuser's cognition and relational architecture, rather than at the level of bystander perception. The accuser is the generative source; the combination that targets the accuser's concealment and the accuser's corrective faculty simultaneously addresses the mechanism at its origin.

**SP + IP:**
SP disruption produces IS-awareness. IP disruption removes bystander authority. Together: the accuser can recognize the substitution (SP) and the social reward for issuing the attribution has been reduced (IP). The combination removes both the internal justification and the external reinforcement.

This combination has a weakness: the DP remains intact. IH is still suppressed in the hierarchical CPT-zone configuration. The accuser has IS-awareness in principle (SP disrupted) but lacks the relational IH to deploy that awareness when the threat is highest (DP intact). SP + IP is tractable in low-threat contexts where RIHG suppression is modest; it is most fragile in precisely the high-threat CPT-zone encounters where the mechanism fires hardest.

**IP + DP:**
IP disruption removes bystander authority. DP disruption restores IH access. Together: the accuser has IH available (DP) and lacks the social reward structure that normally reinforces the attribution (IP). The combination alters both the corrective faculty's availability and the social incentive landscape.

This combination has a weakness: the SP remains intact. The accuser's IH has been restored (DP), but the IS's output continues to arrive as accurate perception (SP). The perceiver who is more epistemically open and less socially rewarded for the attribution may nonetheless continue to experience the IS's output as a genuine perception of arrogance. The IH's openness does not automatically translate into IS-recognition when the SP's concealment remains in the perceptual register.

IP + DP may produce a perceiver who is more IH-disposed and less socially rewarded for attribution, who continues to experience the IS substitution as accurate perception, and who gradually — through the IH faculty's general openness — develops some IS-awareness over time. This is the slowest path to durable dissolution and the most dependent on the DP disruption's IH restoration being strong enough to eventually penetrate the SP's concealment.

### IV.2 The Dominant Minimum Set

SP + DP is the minimum intervention set most likely to produce durable dissolution. The reasoning:

SP disruption (IS-targeted meta-awareness) + DP disruption (RIHG reduction) together give the accuser the specific knowledge of the substitution and the relational IH to act on it. This combination addresses the mechanism at the level of the accuser's cognitive architecture — its concealment and its corrective faculty — rather than at the level of its social expression (IP).

When the SP is disrupted and the DP is disrupted simultaneously, IP loses both of its inputs. The SP provided the sincerity that made the IP's charge appear grounded as authentic assessment rather than defensive posture. The DP's RIHG provided the IH suppression that prevented the accuser from recognizing and modulating the approach-axis energy before it charged the attribution. With SP disrupted (the accuser recognizes the substitution) and DP disrupted (IH is available to reduce approach-axis activation), the attribution is not issued with the same commitment — which means IP's input has been altered at its source. IP reconstitution is thereby blocked without having directly targeted IP at all.

## V. DRS-Level Intervention: The Complete Dissolution Pathway

The TCC's deepest implication is that the most complete dissolution pathway is not any two-paradox combination but an intervention that addresses the DRS directly — the shared generative mechanism that produces all three paradoxes simultaneously.

### V.1 What DRS-Level Intervention Addresses

The DRS is activated by status threat in the CPT zone. Its approach axis produces the IP's charge source. Its avoidance axis produces the SP's concealment substrate. The RIHG it produces in hierarchical configurations produces the DP's suppression. A DRS-level intervention does not target any individual paradox. It alters the conditions under which the DRS activates.

Three categories of DRS-level intervention are distinguishable:

**Threat reduction:** Reducing the status threat that activates the DRS. This could involve changing the categorical pair structures that make the peripheral contribution threatening (altering who is understood to be a legitimate contributor in what domains), changing the comparison proximity (CPT zone specificity), or reducing the identity-stakes attached to the comparison. The challenge: categorical pair structures are encoded at attitudinal (ASM), cultural (CIHMF), and material (NCE) levels that are difficult to alter in ways that transfer to CPT-zone encounters under threat conditions.

**Approach-axis modulation:** Reducing the approach axis's social-reassertion urgency without reducing the avoidance axis, or — more completely — reducing both axes simultaneously. Ji et al.'s (2025) documentation of the DRS's concurrence establishes that the two axes are simultaneous outputs of the same input. Modulating the approach axis while leaving the status threat intact is therefore structurally difficult: the threat continues to activate the full DRS. Effective approach-axis modulation requires either reducing the threat or providing a competing motivational structure that draws the approach-axis energy away from status defense (toward content engagement, which would simultaneously reduce the avoidance axis by providing a competing direction).

**Relational architecture redesign:** Kadodia and Krueger (2026) establish that epistemic injustice is distributed across cognitive agents, cultural practices, and material affordances. NCE-laden environments encode the categorical pair structures that make the peripheral contribution threatening before any individual cognitive process is engaged. Material redesign — altering the physical and procedural architecture in which CPT-zone encounters occur — is the only DRS-level intervention that operates below the mechanism's immune perimeter. By changing the conditions under which status threat activates, material redesign addresses the DRS at its preconditions rather than its outputs.

### V.2 Why DRS-Level Intervention Is Not Always Available

The most complete dissolution pathway is simultaneously the most structurally demanding. DRS-level intervention requires changing the conditions that activate status threat in the CPT zone. These conditions are embedded at three levels:

At the attitudinal level (ASM): the established node's categorical pair beliefs determine the threat intensity threshold. High ASM produces threat at smaller competence differentials. Altering ASM requires the kind of sustained, identity-safe belief revision that is least available in CPT-zone encounters under threat — exactly the condition in which the DP suppresses IH.

At the institutional level (CIHMF): cultural norms that endorse external critique without self-directed IH maintain the collective DRS as a culturally authorized practice. Institutional DRS-level intervention requires a collective recognition that the institution's external-critique apparatus is producing CIHMF — recognition that is invisible from inside the culture that endorses the apparatus as epistemic rigor.

At the material level (NCE): physical and procedural architectures encode categorical pair structures that sustain the DRS's preconditions prior to any individual cognitive process. Material redesign is the most complete DRS-level intervention and the most stable — it operates on structural inertia rather than requiring ongoing motivated change. But it is also the most organizationally costly: changing agendas, credentialing systems, time allocations, and interaction procedures is an architectural intervention, not an epistemic one.

The implication: DRS-level intervention is the most complete dissolution pathway and the least available in the encounters where the mechanism is most active. The minimum viable intervention for most CPT-zone encounters is not the DRS-level but the SP + DP combination — the two-paradox set that addresses the mechanism at the level of the accuser's cognitive architecture.

## VI. The Reconstitution Latency

Single-paradox disruptions fail. But they do not all fail at the same rate. The Reconstitution Latency (RL) describes the interval between disruption and reconstitution — the period during which the partial disruption produces some reduction in the mechanism's operation before the remaining paradoxes rebuild its immunity.

**SP disruption RL:** Reconstitution occurs at the next CPT-zone encounter. The RIHG reconstitutes the SP in the next high-threat configuration regardless of the meta-awareness gained in the prior reflection interval. RL = one encounter. The disruption's benefit is confined to the reflection interval between encounters.

**IP disruption RL:** Reconstitution occurs at the first new social context outside the trained audience. The mechanism's psychological operation is unchanged; only its local social expression has been modified. RL = first new context. In mobile individuals and organizations with diverse stakeholder communities, this may mean very short RL intervals.

**DP disruption RL:** Reconstitution occurs at the first uncontrolled encounter in the hierarchical configuration — the first CPT-zone encounter that occurs outside the specific relational conditions in which the DP disruption was produced. RL = first uncontrolled encounter. Because the RIHG is relational and most structural IH inductions are context-bound, the DP disruption's effect does not automatically transfer to the ambient hierarchical environment.

The RL analysis produces a counterintuitive finding. The paradox whose disruption is most proximate to dissolution (DP) has a shorter RL than the paradox least proximate to dissolution (IP). DP disruption fails faster — at the first uncontrolled encounter — while IP disruption's effects persist longer, because they operate at the social rather than the relational level and are less context-sensitive than the RIHG. The most powerful single disruption reconstitutes fastest because it attacks the mechanism at the level most tightly coupled to the encounter configuration.

This asymmetry has an important intervention design implication: DP disruptions should not be evaluated at the encounter level but at the environmental level. A DP disruption that reduces the RIHG across the institutional environment — rather than in isolated encounters — has a longer RL because the relational conditions sustaining the RIHG have been more thoroughly altered. The SP + DP minimum intervention set is most durable when the DP disruption is environmental rather than encounter-specific.

## VII. Falsifiability: What Disconfirmation Would Require

The TCC is not a merely theoretical claim. It makes specific empirical predictions that distinguish it from the alternative — that the paradoxes are independent and single-paradox interventions are sufficient for durable dissolution.

### VII.1 The Primary Falsifiability Condition

The TCC is disconfirmed if single-paradox interventions targeting any one of the three paradoxes produce durable dissolution equivalent to integrated two-paradox interventions, measured across three or more CPT-zone encounters after the intervention. Durability here means that the mechanism does not reconstitute — that the reduction in arrogance attribution rate is sustained across encounters rather than decaying to baseline.

Specifically:
- SP-only interventions (IS-awareness training without RIHG reduction) that show durable attribution rate reduction across subsequent CPT-zone encounters disconfirm the SP + DP necessity claim
- IP-only interventions (bystander authority training without cognitive-register change) that show durable psychological-level mechanism reduction disconfirm the claim that the mechanism's psychological operation persists under IP disruption alone
- DP-only interventions (RIHG reduction without IS-awareness or IP disruption) that show full mechanism dissolution across uncontrolled encounters disconfirm the SP's role in sustaining the mechanism independently of IH access

### VII.2 What Disconfirmation Would Mean

Disconfirmation of the TCC would not undermine the prior frameworks' core claims. It would mean that the paradoxes, while co-present, do not protect each other in the manner described. The SP, IP, and DP could be independent vulnerabilities that happen to coexist rather than mutually sustaining elements of an integrated architecture. Intervention would then be a simpler problem: address whichever paradox is most tractable given the institutional context.

The TCC's value — if confirmed — is precisely that it changes the intervention design problem. Instead of asking "which paradox should we target?", the question becomes "what is the minimum set of paradoxes that must be targeted simultaneously, and which combination is most tractable in this specific context?" This changes the design requirements for effective intervention in ways that matter practically.

### VII.3 Secondary Predictions That Test Mechanism Integrity

Beyond the primary falsifiability condition, the TCC generates secondary predictions that test whether the reconstitution mechanism operates as specified:

**The RL prediction:** Single-paradox disruptions should show RL patterns consistent with the paradox hierarchy: DP disruption should reconstitute fastest (first uncontrolled encounter), SP disruption next (next CPT-zone encounter), IP disruption slowest (first new social context). If RL patterns contradict this ordering, the reconstitution mechanism account requires revision.

**The synergy prediction:** Two-paradox interventions should show effects larger than the sum of their single-paradox components, because the integrated architecture means each paradox maintains the other two's operation. Disrupting two simultaneously removes more than the sum of what each alone removes. If two-paradox effects are merely additive (equal to sum of single effects), the independence account is partially supported.

**The DRS dominance prediction:** Interventions that reduce status threat in the CPT zone (threat reduction as DRS-level intervention) should show effects across all three paradoxes simultaneously, without specifically targeting any. If DRS-level intervention does not produce concurrent changes in SP, IP, and DP measures, the shared-substrate account is disconfirmed.

## VIII. Predictions

**Prediction I — The Reconstitution Rate**

Single-paradox interventions should show monotonically decaying effects across successive CPT-zone encounters, with the decay rate following the RL hierarchy: DP-only interventions decaying fastest (at the first uncontrolled encounter), SP-only decaying at subsequent encounters, IP-only decaying most slowly but to full baseline in new social contexts. Integrated SP + DP interventions should show non-monotonic, stable effects that do not decay across successive encounters.

*Design:* Randomly assign perceivers to SP-only, IP-only, DP-only, or SP + DP integrated intervention conditions. Measure arrogance attribution rate across five successive CPT-zone encounters following intervention. Predict: single-paradox conditions show attribution-rate decay profiles consistent with RL hierarchy; SP + DP condition shows stable attribution-rate reduction.

**Prediction II — The Reconstitution Input Test**

The Gkinopoulos et al. (2026) finding that willful ignorance is self-reinforcing under identity threat implies that the disruption attempt itself can feed the remaining paradoxes if it activates identity threat. Specifically: SP-only interventions that activate identity threat (confronting the accuser with their IS failure) should show *faster* reconstitution than SP-only interventions delivered in identity-safe conditions — because the identity threat activates the DRS, which restores the SP through the DP's RIHG suppression and restores the IP through the approach axis's intensified activation.

*Design:* Vary SP disruption delivery in identity-threat vs. identity-safe conditions. Measure attribution rate at subsequent encounter. Predict: identity-threatening SP disruption produces faster reconstitution than identity-safe SP disruption, and may produce *higher* attribution rates at the next encounter than no intervention.

**Prediction III — The Dominant Minimum Set Confirmation**

SP + DP integrated interventions should show greater durability than SP + IP and IP + DP integrated interventions, because SP + DP addresses the mechanism at the accuser's cognitive origin (IS-awareness + IH access) and thereby blocks IP reconstitution without directly targeting IP.

*Design:* Randomly assign to SP + DP, SP + IP, and IP + DP conditions. Measure attribution rate and IP proxy (bystander endorsement of attribution) across encounters. Predict: SP + DP shows durable reduction in attribution rate and in IP's bystander endorsement effect; SP + IP shows durable reduction in bystander endorsement but not in attribution rate; IP + DP shows slow, gradual attribution-rate reduction with weaker durability than SP + DP.

**Prediction IV — The DRS Dominance Test**

Structural threat reduction (categorical pair belief change via sustained engagement with peripheral contributors over time) should produce concurrent changes in all three paradox measures — SP (reduced certainty of attribution accuracy), IP (reduced attribution intensity), and DP (reduced RIHG suppression in hierarchical configurations) — without targeting any paradox directly. The shared-substrate account predicts that DRS-level change propagates to all paradox outputs simultaneously.

*Design:* Measure SP, IP, and DP proxy variables before and after a structured long-term peripheral contributor exposure program that does not include explicit paradox-targeted training. Predict: concurrent change across all three paradox measures, with magnitude proportional to ASM reduction (the attitudinal-level DRS precondition).

**Prediction V — The Coherence Test Inversion**

If single-paradox interventions show equivalent durable efficacy to integrated interventions in controlled conditions, the independence account is supported. The TCC predicts this will not occur. But the TCC also predicts where the independence account will most appear to be supported: in low-threat, low-RIHG-suppression conditions (low hierarchical differential, low domain competence proximity). In these conditions, the single paradoxes are less mutually sustaining because the DRS is less fully activated, making single-paradox disruptions more durable. The TCC is most strongly confirmed in high-threat, high-RIHG-suppression CPT-zone conditions.

*Design:* Vary threat level and hierarchical differential orthogonally in a single-vs-integrated intervention comparison. Predict: single-paradox efficacy approaches integrated-paradox efficacy as threat decreases; single-paradox efficacy collapses to zero durability as threat increases and hierarchical differential increases, while integrated efficacy remains stable.

## IX. The Practitioner's Position

The TCC is the most practically demanding conclusion of the research lineage. It does not permit the intervention designs that institutions most readily adopt.

The IH training workshop addresses the DP alone — restoring IH access without altering the SP's concealment or the IP's social authority function. The bias-awareness program addresses the IP alone — changing bystander perception without altering the accuser's cognitive register or the corrective faculty's availability. The structural reform that flattens hierarchy in meeting formats addresses the DP and partially the IP, but without SP disruption the established node continues to experience the IS substitution as accurate perception.

None of these interventions crosses the TCC's threshold alone. Each produces measurable short-term effects. Each reconstitutes at the rate its RL predicts. The institution implementing them can observe the effects and conclude the intervention worked, and then observe the reconstitution and conclude that maintenance is required, and implement the next cycle, and observe the next effect, and observe the next reconstitution — without ever having addressed the minimum intervention set that would produce durable dissolution.

The reconstitution is not implementation failure. It is the architecture delivering its output.

The minimum viable intervention requires IS-targeted meta-awareness training (SP disruption) combined with relational restructuring that reduces the RIHG across the institutional environment — not in isolated encounters but in the ambient conditions of hierarchical CPT-zone interaction (DP disruption at environmental scale). This combination is more demanding than either component alone: it requires changing what the accuser knows about the mechanism they run, and changing the relational conditions in which they encounter the peripheral contribution, simultaneously and in the same institutional environment.

The peripheral node who has received a confident arrogance attribution in a context where neither condition is met has not been failed by the intervention that was tried and reconstituted. They have been failed by the framing that treated the intervention as addressing a single independent vulnerability rather than an integrated immune architecture. The correct framing: what is the mechanism's minimum threshold? What is the smallest simultaneous intervention set that exceeds it? In most institutional contexts, that set is SP + DP. That is the floor. Anything below the floor reconstitutes.

The intervention worked. Then it stopped working. The interval between those two observations is not a measurement of the intervention's half-life. It is a measurement of the reconstitution latency — the time it takes the remaining architecture to rebuild what was partially dismantled. The disruption did not address the mechanism. It addressed one output of the mechanism while the mechanism continued to generate the other two. The mechanism used the disruption interval to consolidate what remained. It always did. The ceiling is not raised by reaching toward one corner of it. The floor is not crossed by one step. What the Triad Coherence Criterion names is the floor. What it specifies is the minimum number of steps required to cross it. The institution that has been intervening below the floor has not been failing to implement the intervention. It has been implementing the wrong one. The architecture was coherent. The intervention was not.
